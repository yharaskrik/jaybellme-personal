Unite

Name: Jay Bell

Email Address: jay@theuniteproject.com

What type of business do you run?: Mobile Activity Platform

What is the name of your company?: Unite

How long have you been in business?: 2 years

Where can we see your amazing work?: http://unite.app.link/AMWtB1v2nE

Facebook Page: http://facebook.com/TheUniteApp/

Instagram Handle: @theuniteteam

Twitter Handle (If applicable): @theuniteteam

What got you started in this industry?: My Co-Founder Jesse Brown split with his girlfriend at the time and wanted to meet people with similar interests, the most popular options out there for meeting new people at the time were strictly dating apps, a la tinder. So he approached me with the idea for a mobile app where people can swipe through fun things to do around them and meeting people with mutual interests. The mobile industry just made sense for this as everyone had a cell phone and desktop/website use is declining.

What was your biggest struggle starting out? How did you overcome this major obstacle?: Our biggest problem we had when we were first starting was finding the right developers. We are a startup so obviously our funding isn't that extensive, so we had to find developers willing to work on the cheap and go through some ups and downs as that is part of the startup space!

To date, what has been your biggest accomplishment with this company? How did you feel after you achieved this accomplishment?: I think that moment where we feel like we had actually done something worry of calling it an accomplishment was when Jesse and I went for a beer and food after this big photo scavenger hunt we put on with the help of the Engineering Society at UBC-O. We were sitting at the bar and we looked across to the people that were sitting at the end of it and they were there scrolling through Unite, pointing and remarking about how cool it was. At that point we just looked at each other and thought, "Yup this is awesome".

Any words of advice you would like to give other startups?: Find the right people! I have written several articles on Medium about finding the right talent to work for your startup, company culture is SO huge when it comes to startups, you cannot bring on people that just want a paycheck or do not work well with the others on the team, since usually the team is so small everyone is working with everyone on a daily basis.
